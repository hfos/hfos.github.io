Deterministic Finite Automata

A Deterministic Finite Automata (DFA) $M$ is a 5-tuple

$$M=(Q, \Sigma , \delta , q_0 ,F)$$

where

  • $Q$ is the set of states;
  • $\Sigma$ is the alphabet;
  • $\delta: Q \times \Sigma \rightarrow Q$ is the transition function;
  • $q_0 \in Q$ is the start time;
  • $F \subseteq Q$ is the set of accepting states.

Language decided by a DFA

Let $M$ be a DFA. Therefore

$$L(M)=\lbrace x\in \Sigma^* :M(x)\text{ accepts}\rbrace \subseteq \Sigma^*$$

is the Language $M$ decides.

Regular languages

A language $L$ is called regular if and only if $L=L(M)$ for some DFA $M$.

Q: Are all languages regular?

A: No.

“Closure” properties:

  • $L_1,L_2$ regular $\Longrightarrow$ $L_1 \cup L_2$ regular

  • $L_1,L_2$ regular $\Longrightarrow$ $L_1 \cdot L_2 = \lbrace xy: x\in L_1 , y \in L_2 \rbrace$ regular

  • $L$ regular $\Longrightarrow$ $L^* = \lbrace x_1 x_2 \dots x_k:k\ge 0 ,\forall i \ x_i \in L \rbrace$ regular

Fact: Starting with $\emptyset$ and $\lbrace a \rbrace$ for each $a \in \Sigma$ can construct any regular language using union, concatenation, star.
